# Cover letter draft

Dear Editor,

We submit **"Organizational-memory claims under original-world grouped
validation: a V4.1 reconciliation"** for methodological review.

This manuscript is a correction and reconciliation of a prepared but
unpublished V4.0 package. No new simulation was run. We identified the exact
canonical V4.0 commit, preserved every release file byte-for-byte, and regenerated
the reported results from committed artifacts.

The central correction concerns the uncertainty procedure. V4.0 resampled
history groups with replacement and relabelled duplicate draws as distinct
cross-validation folds. Under its fixed seed, all 3,000 bootstrap replicates
contained duplicates, allowing exact original-world rows to occur in both
training and testing. V4.1 instead uses simulation seed as the original-world
outer group.

The corrected deep-turnover cumulative-drive point estimate remains positive
(R²=0.695), but the three held-out-world scores are 0.745, 0.414, and 0.925.
The evidence therefore does not certify a lower bound above the predeclared
0.50 threshold. V4.1 withdraws the prior certification while retaining the
narrower observation that globally imposed history-related information remains
detectable in component features after substantial material replacement.

The revision also separates global informational content from local storage.
Because the histories were imposed at whole-world level, and because no
local/neighbour/environment access comparison was committed, local storage is
not established. Tracker survival counts remain reproducible, but event-level
fusion handling cannot be independently reconstructed from the summary artifact.

All numerical changes are classified as unchanged, corrected, or withdrawn.
The submission includes the preserved V4.0 package, machine-readable V4.1
results, updated claim ledger, complete reproducibility commands, and a
page-by-page visual QA report.

We believe the reconciliation is useful precisely because it changes the
scientific verdict rather than defending the earlier headline. We welcome
independent methodological review.

Sincerely,

Tommy Lepesteur
