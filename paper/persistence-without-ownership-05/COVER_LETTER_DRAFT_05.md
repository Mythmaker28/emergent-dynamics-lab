Dear Editors,

Please consider our manuscript, **“Causal Persistence Without Ownership: Deep Material Turnover Dissociates an Engineered State from Individual Memory in Simulated Droplets,”** as a methods-and-concepts study in artificial life and complex systems.

The manuscript asks a narrow question that is often blurred in discussions of organizational persistence: if an experience-dependent state survives replacement and remains causally active, has individual memory been demonstrated? We answer prospectively in a frozen reaction–transport droplet model. Twenty-one of 50 one-shot primary worlds met all preregistered feasibility, tracking, assay, and deep-turnover criteria. Own-history information survived and the engineered state retained a specific local causal feeding effect. Nevertheless, target-local information failed two preregistered exclusion comparisons, so the frozen decision tree selected “causal feeding effect without ownership.” The result is a direct dissociation: causal persistence is insufficient for individual memory under the tested criteria.

We believe the study is useful because the negative gate is the main result, not a caveat appended to a positive story. The manuscript separates decodability, causal expression, material replacement, intervention locality, and ownership. It also exposes the full correction chain that motivated the final design: leakage across original-world groups, a component-tracking failure mode, a fusion incident, a downgrade of historical uncertainty, an authorization-template defect caught before execution, a hash-bound one-shot prospective family, and an independent raw-only reimplementation that matched 9,357 terminal values exactly.

The claims are deliberately bounded. We do not claim life, agency, identity, active reconstruction, reproduction, heredity, evolution, or absence of distributed encoding. The feeding readout is directly engineered, and the result applies to the frozen C1c architecture and operational ownership criterion. Earlier V4/V4.1 materials remain preserved; this V5 manuscript coexists with that correction history while serving as the primary external-review synthesis.
