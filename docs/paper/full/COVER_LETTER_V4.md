# Cover letter (V4 draft — not sent)

Dear Editor,

We submit "From Material Turnover to Causal Memory: A Prospective Falsification Ladder for Organizational
Continuity in a Simulated Droplet" for consideration as a methods-and-results contribution.

We report one robust positive and an honest negative boundary in a simulated reaction-transport droplet. The
positive: under a globally imposed history, a continuously tracked droplet retains an engineered
one-dimensional cumulative causal memory through substantial constituent turnover (held-out **R² = 0.89, 95%
CI [0.84, 0.96]** at old-material fraction M ≈ 0.19, 100% track survival, 0 reassignment switches), causally
expressed (necessity established) and transplantable. The negative: a second decodable coordinate does not
qualify as an independent, causal, turnover-surviving dimension — under the canonical analysis it remains
below the qualification threshold at deep turnover (**R² = −0.24, 95% CI [−0.78, 0.32]**) — and the memory
does not individuate droplets.

All longitudinal held-out numbers are the outputs of a committed, deterministic reproduction pipeline
(`python -m reproduction.primary`), byte-identical in a clean-room rerun; these are prospective, held-out
trajectories analysed with a retrospectively standardized, fully committed pipeline. An earlier,
non-committed analysis produced different headline point estimates that we could not regenerate; that history
is documented transparently in `HEADLINE_NUMBER_ERRATUM.md`, and no conclusion depends on it.

The paper's methodological value is a prospective *falsification ladder* with kill-switches that separates
decodability, storage, causal expression, turnover survival, and continuity-safe tracking — and a transparent
correction of a largest-component reselection tracking artefact exposed by human visual inspection. We make no
claim of life, agency, identity, individuation, reproduction, or heredity. All code, sealed manifests, raw
data, and figure generators are available; negative results and corrections are retained.

We confirm the work is original, not under review elsewhere, and involves no human or animal subjects.

Sincerely, the authors.
