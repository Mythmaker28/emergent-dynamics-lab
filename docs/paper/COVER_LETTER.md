# Cover letter (draft — not sent)

Dear Editor,

We submit "From Material Turnover to Causal Memory: A Falsification Ladder for Organizational Continuity in a
Simulated Droplet" for consideration as a methods-and-results contribution.

We report one robust positive and an honest negative boundary in a simulated reaction-transport droplet. The
positive: under a globally imposed history, a continuously tracked droplet retains an engineered
one-dimensional cumulative causal memory through substantial constituent turnover (held-out $R^2=0.98$, 95% CI
[0.97,1.00] at old-material fraction $M\approx0.19$, 100% track survival), causally expressed and
transplantable. The negative: a second decodable coordinate fails to qualify as an independent, causal,
turnover-surviving dimension, and the memory does not individuate droplets.

The paper's methodological value is a preregistered *falsification ladder* with kill-switches that separates
decodability, storage, causal expression, turnover survival, and continuity-safe tracking — and a transparent
correction of a largest-component reselection tracking artefact exposed by human visual inspection. We make no
claim of life, agency, identity, individuation, reproduction, or heredity. All code, sealed manifests, raw
data, and figure generators are available; negative results and corrections are retained.

We confirm the work is original, not under review elsewhere, and involves no human or animal subjects.

Sincerely, the authors.
