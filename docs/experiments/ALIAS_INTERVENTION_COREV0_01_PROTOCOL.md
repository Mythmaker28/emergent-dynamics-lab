## 1. Question and scope

For a screening-eligible CORE V0 candidate organization on laws {0, 52}, does its phenotype continuity `P`
under constituent turnover `M` reflect an organization **carried by its constituents** (individuality) or a
**stationary spatial-occupancy / look-alike alias** bound to a site?

This test is designed to REJECT (or fail to reject) the stationary-occupancy / sparse look-alike alias that
0/47 direct EXP02 rows could reject. It does **not** test internal self-repair after structural disruption
(a distinct future perturbation). No claim about memory, life, organism, or autopoiesis is made. A negative
result stays negative; thresholds are not loosened.

## 2. Frozen substrate and observers (unchanged CORE V0)

WorldSpec(n_particles=64, n_types=3, box_size=1.0, initial_speed=0.04); dt=0.02; backend vectorized (validated
against scalar reference). DetectionSpec(connection_radius=0.11, min_size=4). PhenotypeSpec(length_scale=0.11,
speed_scale=0.25). TrackerSpec(max_centroid_distance=0.16, min_size_ratio=0.25). Probe thresholds P>0.8, M<0.5.
Laws generated by the frozen `law_from_halton` (skip 32). Mutation / neighbor type-transition / recycling OFF.

## 3. Enrollment rule (uses the EXACT frozen eligibility; no new looser rule)

For each (law, seed): simulate 600 steps (snapshot interval 10). Build the tracker at cadences {10,30,60} and
measure P,M at lags {1,3,6}. An endpoint (start_step,end_step) is **eligible** iff it is probe-positive
(P>0.8, M<0.5) on a **track-clean** (no split/merge/ambiguous event on that track) **long** (>=8 observations)
track under **>=2 cadences** — identical to the frozen `audit_candidates` cross-cadence rule that defined the
EXP02 and HOLDOUT04 dispositions. The run ENROLLS at the **first eligible endpoint** (smallest end_step, then
smallest start_step). The candidate = the clean-long track owning that endpoint (most observations, then
smallest track id); `phi*` = that entity's phenotype at end_step; `C` = its constituent diagnostic IDs;
`old_centroid` = its periodic centroid; `S*` = the full 64-particle state at end_step (the shared pre-intervention state).
A seed with no eligible endpoint before the horizon is **censored** (recorded, never deleted).

Independent verification (frozen as a test): this rule reproduces HOLDOUT04 exactly — law 0 enrolls {3002,3004},
law 52 enrolls {3001,3003}, all other holdout seeds censored.

## 4. Experimental unit (anti-pseudoreplication)

**First eligible endpoint per seed per law** = one unit. Recorded per unit: law, seed, first_eligible_end,
prior snapshots, candidate size, enrollment status. Seeds without an endpoint are censored, not dropped.

## 5. Same-state matched branches (identical pre-intervention state S*)

```
                      S*  (full physical state at t* = first_eligible_end)
                       |
     ------------------+------------------+------------------
     |                 |                  |                 |
  CONTROL            SHAM             PERTURBED           PLACEBO
 continue        pipeline no-op    displace candidate   displace matched
```

All branches simulate `horizon_post = 360` steps from S* (36 base-cadence snapshots).

## 6. Primary perturbation (ONE, pre-declared)

Rigid off-site translation of the **entire** candidate constituent set `C` by a fixed vector Δ = (0.30, 0.30)
box units (mod box). Chosen before inspecting any causal result.

- PHYSICAL PROPERTY CHANGED: absolute positions of `C` (centroid moves by |Δ|min-image = 0.424, > tracker gate
  0.16 and > detection radius 0.11). The candidate's spatial relationship to the other 58 particles changes.
- QUANTITIES CONSERVED: particle count (64); per-type counts; every particle velocity (hence total momentum);
  the candidate's internal minimum-image geometry (rigid translation) and therefore its phenotype `phi*` at
  t*+0; the other 58 particles' positions/velocities.
- EXPECTED IMMEDIATE EFFECT: the candidate reappears intact at the new site c+Δ with phenotype ≈ phi*; the
  tracker cannot stitch old→new by centroid (0.424 > 0.16), so a fresh tracker sees a birth at the new site.
- WHY STATIC / OCCUPANCY ALIAS SHOULD FAIL OR DIFFER: if the "entity" is a fixed site (attractor basin), the
  displaced constituents have no basin at c+Δ and disperse, while the OLD site is re-occupied by turnover
  particles reforming phi* with low overlap to `C`.
- EXPECTED LOOK-ALIKE RESPONSE: continuity that depended on ambiguous association appears as
  births/ambiguous/split/merge in the branch lineage, and as an old-site entity with high P but low material
  overlap with `C`.
- WHAT WOULD SUPPORT DYNAMICAL RE-ESTABLISHMENT / INDIVIDUALITY: the displaced `C` sustains phi* at the new
  site for a sustained window **beyond** the matched PLACEBO, while the old site does **not** regenerate phi*.

## 7. Sham and placebo (controls)

- SHAM (Δ=0): exercises the full detect→select→serialize→duplicate→reinstantiate→resume path with no physical
  change. Proven non-perturbing: SHAM equals CONTROL bit-for-bit over the full horizon (unit tests). Controls
  the intervention machinery; it is not "control renamed" (mechanically distinct, physically null). If SHAM ever
  diverges from CONTROL, the harness is confounded and the run is invalid.
- PLACEBO (mandatory active control): translate a size-comparable **non-candidate** connected component by the
  same Δ. Controls generic "displace a clump into a new neighborhood" effects (inertia + new-site collision).
  The organization-specific claim requires PERTURBED to **exceed** PLACEBO.

## 8. Primary endpoints (measured separately; NO composite score)

Per branch, per post-intervention snapshot: (1) best P to phi* anywhere; (2) carrier P to phi* (carrier =
detected entity with max constituent-ID overlap with `C`; IDs used only for measurement); (3) carrier M =
Jaccard(C, carrier); (4) carrier size; (5) carrier centroid distance to NEW and (6) to OLD site; (7) old-site
entity P and (8) its material overlap with `C`; (9) new-site entity P and (10) overlap; (11) initial centroid
displacement; (12) branch lineage flags (births after start, ambiguous, split, merge); (13) matched contrasts
PERTURBED−CONTROL, PERTURBED−SHAM, PERTURBED−PLACEBO on carrier persistence and old-site regeneration.

Derived (logged, not composited): carrier_persist_consecutive; carrier_at_new_site_snapshots;
old_site_regen_any (old-site entity with P>0.8 AND material overlap with C < 0.5).

## 9. Recovery rule (comparative — absolute persistence is NOT evidence)

A branch shows **audited re-establishment** only if ALL hold: (A) a real initial off-site displacement
(|Δ| > tracker gate); (B) the carrier sustains P>0.8 at the NEW site for a sustained consecutive window;
(C) that window **exceeds** the matched PLACEBO; (D) the OLD site does not regenerate phi*; (E) no unresolved
ambiguous-association / look-alike explanation in the branch lineage. Anything failing E, or explained by
old-site regeneration / spatial occupancy / ambiguous association, is **ALIAS-COMPATIBLE RECOVERY**, not RECOVERY.

## 10. Alias audit (explicit)

Flag and classify: track-id changes; a different entity becoming best match; compatible concurrent association
edges; split; merge; disappearance then independent reappearance; old-site re-occupation by another aggregate;
centroid-only continuity; turnover combined with loss and later independent look-alike. Any apparent recovery
depending on these is ALIAS-COMPATIBLE.

## 11. Frozen falsifiers

- F1: PERTURBED does not differ from SHAM and CONTROL on the pre-declared endpoints.
- F2: apparent recoveries are explained by look-alike / tracker reassignment / occupancy (old-site regeneration
  with turnover constituents), i.e. do not exceed PLACEBO.
- F3: the survivors do not reproduce an eligible endpoint on the fresh causal seeds (enrollment fails).
- F4: the perturbation produces no real initial displacement (|Δ| ≤ tracker gate) → NON-INFORMATIVE INTERVENTION.
- F5: the perturbation destroys the candidate everywhere → CATASTROPHIC INTERVENTION (tests destruction, not
  re-establishment).

## 15. Readout correction (D-016, pre-results)

The frozen decision rule (§8–9) is comparative: PERTURBED must exceed PLACEBO and the old site must not
regenerate. The first harness implementation measured the placebo by following the candidate (not the displaced
non-candidate clump) and used a transient-fragile consecutive metric. Corrected before any results commit
(D-016): each branch follows the set it actually displaced, against that set's own phenotype and home site, with
a robust late-window re-establishment metric; "individuality under turnover" additionally requires the displaced
candidate to re-establish while NOT retaining ~all constituents (M→1.0 rigid-cluster translation is trivial
translation-covariance, excluded). Design, enrollment, perturbation, branches, and thresholds are unchanged.
