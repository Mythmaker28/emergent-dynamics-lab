## Separation architecture

The frozen Stage-A engine does not import the instrumentation module. The detector reads only instantaneous `m`
and periodic lattice geometry. It does not read `b`, resource, history, passive labels, tracker state, activity,
throughput, future classifications, or intervention quantities. Components therefore exist only as passive
measurement records and cannot feed physics.

Tracking consumes detector outputs only. Every candidate association edge logs raw overlap, dilated overlap,
periodic centroid distance, area ratio, score and qualification. Raw-overlap graphs retain split and merge
structure. A unique mutual-best rule may bridge a nonoverlapping translation. One-frame many-to-one-to-many contact
is never bridged retrospectively: it is marked unresolved. Temporary contact is logged only while two same-frame
components remain detector-distinct at a one-cell gap, so the event itself cannot fuse or duplicate identities.
General many-to-many association is unresolved rather than optimized away. Every raw association edge records its
qualification, selection status and reason. Symmetric qualified geometry ties are carried into an explicit
ambiguity graph and cannot be re-labelled as resolved dissolution plus appearance.

## Turnover

For an enrolled component, `q` is initialized as its current matter on its support and zero elsewhere. It is
advected by the already-computed gross directional matter flows, proportional to the labelled fraction at each
source. `q` never feeds the engine, detector, tracker or classifier except through the final declared retention
diagnostic. The identities `sum(q_next)=sum(q)` and `0<=q_next<=m_next` are checked at every advance.

This distinguishes replacement from gross boundary traffic. The synthetic full-swap fixture drives retention to
zero while matter support persists; a reverse swap returns the original cohort and retention returns to one.
Cumulative flux would incorrectly count both as two replacements, so it is forbidden as the turnover estimator.

## Diagnostics and non-circularity

Activity is ordinary gross matter transport. Energetic throughput is ordinary gross bond formation work plus gross
rupture release. Geometry, winding, ingress, egress and resource exchange are raw diagnostics. Mean `b` may be
logged, but no `b` threshold, permeability/conductance value, cut, or intervention response can detect, associate,
or classify a Stage-B candidate.

The regime classifier is total and has a frozen precedence. Its raw assembler exact-checks reported area and
percolation against the detector and refuses duplicate, orphan, missing, nonfinite or invalid observations. Its
numerical thresholds and the mapping from raw track records to summary values must be committed in the B1 manifest
before any DEV initialization.
