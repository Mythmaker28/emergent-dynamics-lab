## Audit-only predictions checked

- Large erase effects and substantial ambient no-history memory: already observed in Phase 0.7, compatible with
  `H_RELATIVE` but non-unique.
- Continuous history labels recover without future feeding: yes, exact `(a1,a2)`, dose, and order reproduce from the
  seed and code.
- Assigned binary history contrast: absent from the implementation.
- Retroactive midpoint deep `m_plus` difference: sign `+---` across the four deep-valid worlds.
- Retroactive order-sign deep `m_plus` difference: available in only three worlds, sign `+-+`; world 50005 lacks the
  negative-order condition.

These last two diagnostics are reported to show why relabelling is not a hidden rescue. They are not hypothesis
tests and may not be used to choose a prospective history definition.
