# EXP-SC-HISTORY-MATERIAL-CONTINUITY-00 — PROTOCOL (preregistered)

Frozen configuration: `edlab/experiments/sc_hmc/config.py`. The physics is imported unchanged from the
frozen EXP-SC line; only β = 0.10 is selected (EXP-SC-00B), never retuned.

## Question and claim scope
Can a mesoscopic organization retain causally meaningful continuity through material turnover, and does
its continuous history predict its future better than material composition or instantaneous phenotype?
Maximum permitted positive claim: *organizational continuity / history dependence / causal persistence*
through material replacement — never identity, consciousness, life, individuality, or transfer to
physical droplets.

## Entity / partition ontology
Candidate X_t = the largest connected component of `rho > 0.30·rho_max` (≥ 12 cells), detected by
`scaffold.observables.detect`. Detection is **ID-free** (threshold + periodic connected components; no
historical tracker). Partition robustness is checked at thresholds {0.25, 0.30, 0.35}.

## Material continuity M(t0,t) — passive pulse-chase
At t0 the cohort field is collapsed to 2 passive bins: bin0 = all material present at t0 ("labelled"),
bin1 = material fed after t0 ("new"). Bins advect/decay with rho and never enter the physics.
`M(t0,t) = labelled / (labelled + new)` over the entity mask. Reported with labelled-leaving,
new-entering, total mass, and partition dependence.

## Organizational axes (separate; no composite)
- P1 geometry/topology (size, rg, anisotropy eigenvalues, compactness, core fraction — translation/rotation invariant)
- P2 internal U/V state (ID-independent phenotype [het, n_domains, interface, radial_u, janus] + mean σ) — privileged, existence-assay only
- P3 function (specific uptake + efficiency + throughput)
- P4 causal response (frozen N/c intervention battery difference profile)
- P5 recovery (reconvergence to a co-evolved control after a bounded perturbation)
- P6 path (uninterrupted admissible-intermediate continuity; ID-free, overlap/jump based)
**No material label enters any organizational axis (G8).**

## Intervention battery (external handles only)
Bounded pulses held 5 steps then released: N_boost (+0.5·N0), N_deprive (×0.3), c_boost (+0.5),
c_suppress (×0.3). Response = perturbed minus matched unperturbed control over 120 steps (the matched
control is the K10 intervention-null).

## Counterfactual arms at t_c
- H continuous history (the entity itself)
- P phenotype-matched reset (preserve rho/c/N/geometry/mass/position; randomize internal U/V history)
- M material-retaining organizational reset (preserve rho AND cohort labels exactly; spatially permute U/V)
- U unrelated phenotype-matched entity (different seed, matched geometry, no shared history)
- C exact full-state clone under independent bounded environmental noise → stochastic ceiling

## Splits (committed before tuning)
Development seeds 9401–9412; prospective hold-out 9501–9516; unrelated pool 9601–9612. Stratified by
seed, checkpoint age, material band, internal regime, intervention family. Internal init = "random"
(organization must self-organize). Timing: warmup 2000, t0 relabel, checkpoint t_c = 700 steps, early
reference at t_c − 350.

## Primary positive pattern (all required)
1. M(t0,t_c) ≤ M_low = 0.35; 2. ≥ 3 organizational axes within the continuous-history regime while
matched controls fail; 3. history outpredicts phenotype reset; 4. history outpredicts material reset;
5. exact clone sets the stochastic ceiling; 6. survives prospective; 7. no tracker/cohort label enters
the organizational score.

## Development gates G1–G12 → freeze → prospective gates
All G-gates must pass before the prospective split is executed. A negative result is valid; the
substrate is not repaired automatically.
