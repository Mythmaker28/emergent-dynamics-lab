# FINGERPRINT CLAIM SCOPE

## The claim, and nothing wider

> A frozen, standardized causal-response fingerprint can remain **stable** under microscopically different but
> behaviourally equivalent implementations, while **distinguishing** systems that differ in reachable function or
> in genuine hidden state.

This is a **new prospective claim**, not a rescue or a regrading of the failed hierarchy benchmark (D-069).

## Explicitly OUTSIDE the claim

**Model minimality.** The fingerprint contains no model. It cannot be wrong about one.
**Architecture discovery.** No graph, no boundary, no component is inferred or asserted.
**Lineage identity.** The fingerprint says nothing about whether two systems are the same *individual*.

## The fingerprint never says SAME

Its verdicts are `INDISTINGUISHABLE_UNDER_REPERTOIRE` · `DIFFERENT` · `INDETERMINATE`.

Two systems that no admissible probe can separate form an **equivalence class**. Calling them one individual is an
assertion the measurement does not license, and this programme exists because that assertion is easy to make and
hard to retract.

## What is excluded from the fingerprint, and why it is load-bearing

Excluded **completely**: transducer-class labels · selected lag sets · model complexity · `DELAYED_STATIC` /
`FINITE_HISTORY` or any such name · inferred minimal architecture · evaluator-supplied component identities ·
active-planner intervention sequences · tracker IDs.

**A redundant lag must not create a fingerprint difference when all measured responses are identical.** That was the
defect that retired the previous observer. The exclusion is not assumed to work: control **L1** puts the class label
and lag set *back in* and shows the false difference reappear (`d`: 0.0000 → 0.0612). The exclusion is
load-bearing, and it is proved to be.

## Two verdicts that are facts about the world, not failures of the method

**Equivalence is relative to a repertoire.** Under **rich** access the redundant-lag system (`xor3`) *is*
separable — its reconvergent paths **glitch** on a register step. Under the **droplet** repertoire, which has no
register step, it is not. Both statements are true; neither is a label.

**Silence is not a fingerprint.** A saturated gate answers nothing to any admissible droplet probe. Its all-zero
fingerprint is reported as `INDETERMINATE`, never matched against another silent system.
