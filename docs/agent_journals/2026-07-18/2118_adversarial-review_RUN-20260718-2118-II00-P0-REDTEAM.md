### Independent formal requirements

For substrate state `S_t`, natural transition `F`, extensive quantities `Q`, and a physical boundary-edge extractor
`B(S_t)`, interventional individuality is scientifically askable only if all of the following hold:

1. `B` is computed from instantaneous local physical variables under a translation/permutation-equivariant rule;
   no tracker, diagnostic material ID, outcome, component label, interior bit, or permanent entity state enters
   physics or the boundary definition.
2. A connected bounded component and its boundary persist longitudinally while passive crossing ledgers establish
   turnover; association uncertainty and every split/merge edge remain explicit.
3. Local and environmental ports change transition edges after `t0`, not `S_t0`, and their supports are frozen from
   pre-intervention physics. Open coefficients satisfy `F_11(S)=F(S)` exactly for every finite state.
4. The local/environment compilers are idempotent and commute. Later A/B masks are disjoint or have an explicit
   overlap stop and also commute; no tracker feedback recentres them.
5. Every internal edge exchange is antisymmetric: `J_xy^Q=-J_yx^Q`. Therefore
   `sum_x Delta Q_x = reservoir_Q + reaction_Q + controller_Q`; boundary work and controller work are separate raw
   ledger terms. Exact accounting of nonzero controller work does not itself make the manipulation admissible.
6. Four valid `L0E0/L1E0/L0E1/L1E1` rows exist. Their matrix `[1,l,e,l*e]` has determinant one and rank four; a
   local A/B factorial remains a later, separate rank-four experiment.
7. Candidate support and response are defined from ordinary physical fields. A reporter introduced specifically
   for a history state, or a port whose coefficient creates the only boundary, fails exclusion.

These are necessary conditions for an identifiable experiment, not evidence that any natural entity or causal
individual exists.

### Anti-manufacture counterexamples

- **Detector-cut counterexample:** take any dense particle subset selected by a connected-component threshold and
  set every crossing contact coefficient to zero. The subset becomes perfectly isolated even if the natural law
  had no physical boundary. Repeating this construction for arbitrary subsets shows that addressability was
  manufactured by the detector-defined cut.
- **One-sided-flux counterexample:** if an intervened destination uses `J_xy*` while the other endpoint retains
  `-J_xy`, then the global residual is `J_xy*-J_xy`. Logging it exactly does not restore conservation or exclusion;
  this is the accepted Stage-A failure in general form.
- **Phase-contour counterexample:** a Cahn-Hilliard droplet can retain a closed level set after all interior
  organization is scrambled. Its persistence proves a phase-separated pattern/material component, not an
  operational or causally addressable individual.
- **Membrane-label counterexample:** if a bond update receives a closure reward, component-size target, inside/outside
  bit, or preformed ring mask, then a closed membrane is encoded by the law. A later effect of cutting those bonds
  cannot establish emergent individuality.

### Candidate comparison

**A — phase-field droplet.** A conserved finite-volume phase field can yield a closed diffuse interface, turnover,
and natural split/merge. Antisymmetric face fluxes are possible. However, entity membership depends on a contour
convention, Cahn-Hilliard chemical potentials have a wider stencil than a face, and a semipermeability factor tied
to the contour risks making every phase domain an operational compartment by construction. It is computationally
moderate and dataclass-compatible, but its L/E edge inventory and anti-manufacture exclusion are weakest.

**B — contact-edge particle aggregate.** Contacts and aggregates can form without an entity label, while explicit
pairwise `J_ij=-J_ji` exchanges give the cleanest material ledger and natural split/merge. It is closest to the
existing LawSpec/trackers. Without a separate physical surface state, though, the aggregate boundary is only a
detector graph cut; gating that cut manufactures a compartment. Particle IDs also invite material/identity
conflation, and neighbour-list cost plus contact churn are substantial. Adding a physical boundary-bond state to
repair this limitation largely turns B into C.

**C — lattice matter with emergent membrane bonds.** A uniform local bond-assembly law can produce physical
high-resistance faces, and all crossings can use a single antisymmetric value applied to both endpoints. Boundary
components are defined from the physical bond graph rather than a tracker, turnover remains possible with positive
leak permeability, and split/merge/dissolution are native bond-graph events. Cost is moderate and a new frozen
LawSpec-style dataclass is straightforward. The load-bearing risk is that the bond field itself may be an encoded
membrane. It is acceptable only if no closure/topology term exists and the same law admits open sheets, leaks,
percolation, dissolution, fusion, and no-boundary worlds.

### Minimal conservative construction worth specifying

On an undirected lattice graph, store site matter `rho_x`, mobile boundary monomer `a_x`, ordinary environmental
solute `n_x`, and an undirected bond fraction `b_xy=b_yx`. There is no history state in the vertical slice.
Use a uniform local assembly rate `R_xy` with no component/topology input, conserve boundary material by adding
`+R_xy` to `b_xy` and matching `-R_xy/2` to each endpoint monomer, and log any fuel/work reservoir separately.
For every transported extensive field use one face value
`J_xy^q=K_q(b_xy)*(mu_x^q-mu_y^q)=-J_yx^q`, applying `-dt*J_xy^q` and `+dt*J_xy^q` to the two endpoints. Require
`K_q(b)>0` on the natural path so turnover is possible. Passive crossing cohorts may copy these exact transfers but
must never enter `R`, `K`, response, detection, or association.

A later environmental port may only scale an already-existing selected membrane-face flux once and apply the same
scaled value with opposite signs at both endpoints. A destination-only parent substitution repeats the no-go. A
zero-flux arm is interpretable only as necessity of the declared membrane-crossing bundle; it does not prove
natural autonomy, and matched off-boundary/random-edge controls must fail to mimic it. A later local port may delete
only an ordinary pre-existing history-state-to-natural-response edge after that state has independently earned its
place in the substrate. No new history reporter is admissible.
