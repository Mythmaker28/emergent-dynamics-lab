# EXP-SC-00B — identity exists, and it does not persist (2026-07-12)

The corrected criterion was the right one. With O2' asking for **viability** instead of **size-invariance**, the
substrate qualified prospectively at `beta = 0.10` on unseen seeds — and the u-state and v-state droplets do have
visibly different body sizes (24 entities vs 11), exactly as an uptake coupling must produce. The old O2 would have
killed a substrate that was working, for doing the thing it was designed to do. That was my error and the user caught
it.

**O4' is the best result this project has produced.** The internal state predicts a droplet's *future* nutrient
uptake with a partial correlation of **+0.52** (p < 0.0005) **after regressing out mass, radius, density and size**.
Identity is causally efficacious and is not a disguised proxy for being big. The metric that establishes this is one
I first had to prove could *fail*: the must-fail unit case is a confound where a naive correlation fires at |r| > 0.8
and the partial correlation must not — and doesn't.

Then R8-B killed it. Prototypes fitted on the early half re-identify entities in the late half at 0.286 against a
chance of 0.083. That is 3.4x chance — a real signal — and the preregistered bar was chance + 0.25 = 0.333. It misses.
I am not moving the bar. I have watched this project die eight times to keep bars where they were put.

The reason is one number appearing twice: **within-entity drift is 0.394, between-entity distance is 0.836.** An
entity wanders nearly half as far from itself over time as it sits from its neighbours. The internal organizations
are diverse (R8-A scrapes through at 2.12) but they **coarsen and rearrange** while the material beneath them is
replaced. So a droplet at late times no longer resembles its own younger self more than it resembles the others.

**Identity exists here. It just doesn't hold still.** That is a different failure from every previous one — not
"there is nothing there", but "there is something there and it does not last". Nine substrates in, that is progress,
and it names the next requirement exactly: the internal organization must be **pinned**, not merely multistable.

And I did not reach for `D_int`. Lowering internal diffusion would very likely pin the domains and push R8-B over the
bar. It would also be tuning-after-failure on the exact parameter the failure points to, which is the one move this
project exists to refuse. The pinning mechanism goes in the next protocol, declared before it is run.
