# EXP-SC-00 — separating the degrees of freedom was not enough (2026-07-12)

The architecture did what it was designed to do. The scaffold coheres with the internal network switched **off**
(O1). The internal fields cannot manufacture a droplet (O3). And flipping a droplet's internal state changes its
**future** uptake by up to 28 %, while the `beta = 0` control changes it by **exactly zero** (O4) — identity is
causally efficacious, not a decorative label. Three of four orthogonality criteria, clean.

O2 killed it. Different internal states do not leave localization intact — entity radius of gyration swings by up to
4 cells against a 1.5-cell tolerance — and it fails at **every** coupling strength in the grid, on unseen seeds. At
the weakest coupling, O2 still fails *and* O4 fails as well. There is no window.

The reason is one sentence, and it is the finding: **uptake feeds growth, and growth feeds the scaffold's mass
balance.** The internal state changes how fast the droplet eats; how fast it eats changes how big it is; how big it
is *is* its localization. I separated cohesion and identity into different degrees of freedom — and then coupled
identity to behaviour through the one channel that builds the body.

EXP-MA-00 shared the transport flux. EXP-SC-00 shares the mass balance. **Separating the degrees of freedom is not
enough; the couplings have to be separated too.** That is a sharper statement of the requirement than I had when the
turn started, and it points somewhere specific: identity must act on something that does not feed the scaffold —
where the droplet *goes*, when it *fires*, how it *responds to a probe*. Not how much it eats.

## The near-miss I want on the record
At my hand-set `beta = 0.6`, O2 failed. I scanned `beta` and found 0.15 passing both O2 and O4 — on the very seed
the failure had been observed on. That number was sitting there, and adopting it would have been the single thing
this whole project exists to not do. So I re-declared `beta` as *prospectively selected* by the qualification, on a
frozen grid and unseen seeds, and ran it.

**`beta = 0.15` fails O2 on the unseen seed.** The window did not replicate. Had I taken the post-hoc value I would
have walked a non-qualifying substrate straight into R8 and, most likely, reported something.

Eight substrates, eight negatives, nothing promoted. The gates keep firing earlier and the reasons keep getting
sharper. That is the only thing here that is actually accumulating.
