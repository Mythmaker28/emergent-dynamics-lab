**Result: QUANTUM HARDWARE NOT USED.** IBM Quantum is not used by default; only a bounded relevance
audit was performed, as required.

A quantum experiment is authorized only if the classical study produces a *precise* task such as:
distinguishing coherent vs incoherent history dependence; comparing information gained per unit
perturbation; a formally defined quantum channel-discrimination analogue; or an adaptive weak-measurement
schedule versus an optimal classical baseline.

**The classical study produced no such task.** The decisive classical finding is a *null*: the
continuous-history entity carries no history-specific causal signature (per-axis discrimination at chance
vs unrelated entities; individuation AUC 0.52 for causal response; no predictive model beats the mean
baseline). There is therefore:
- no coherent-vs-incoherent history-dependence contrast to sharpen (there is no measurable history
  dependence to begin with);
- no channel-discrimination instance (the "channels" induced by H, P, M, U are not classically
  separable, so a quantum discriminator has nothing to beat);
- no adaptive-measurement advantage to test (the classical response is already reproducible within its
  stochastic ceiling; the missing signal is absence, not measurement noise).

A QPU cannot replace a missing classical observable or manufacture material-turnover evidence. Per the
kill-switch discipline, **no circuit was defined, no shots were budgeted, and no hardware was run.**
Should a future PINNED-internal-organization substrate produce a genuine, classically-marginal
history-vs-snapshot contrast, a channel-discrimination formulation would be the first candidate to
revisit — but only then.
